On Beginnings (Case Study)

Kuhu Juneja

Every day is a new beginning, and there is no real end. Life is a continuous cycle of moments, each one building upon the last and leading to the next. As I sit down to write this personal narrative, I am reminded of this truth. I want this memoir to have a homely and warm feel, like a hug from an old friend.

It is a "memo intertwined between beginnings and endings, poetry and prose." I want to tell my story in a way that captures the essence of my experiences. Handwritten elements will speak to the core of my memories, drawing attention to the moments that have shaped me.

All memories are not linear and don't follow a chronological order. They are all important, and all text applies equally to all images. To emphasize this, I will push the images to the left, allowing the majority of the width to be white space. This draws attention to the content and weight of these memories.


Through this personal narrative, I hope to share a piece of myself with the world. Life is a beautiful and complex journey, and I want to capture its essence in all its glory. This is my story, and I am excited to share it with you.
